Our History

Journey of KARE ACM SIGBED Student Chapter

History of KARE ACM SIGBED

The KARE ACM SIGBED (Special Interest Group on Embedded Systems) Student Chapter was established to foster research, innovation, and collaboration in the field of embedded systems at Kalasalingam Academy of Research and Education. Our chapter serves as a platform for students to engage with cutting-edge technologies, participate in competitions, and contribute to the advancement of embedded systems research.

Our Journey

Grand Inauguration

June 19, 2025

The KARE ACM SIGBED Student Chapter was officially inaugurated, marking the beginning of our journey to empower students in embedded systems research and development.

Chapter Inauguration

July 14, 2025

The official chapter inauguration ceremony was held, bringing together faculty, students, and industry professionals to celebrate the establishment of the SIGBED chapter at KARE.

First Major Event – DigiCon 3.0

October 2025

We successfully organized DigiCon 3.0, a multi-phase technical competition that brought together participants from various institutions to showcase their skills in embedded systems and related technologies.

Our Mission

To create a vibrant community of embedded systems enthusiasts, promote research and innovation, and provide opportunities for students to develop their technical skills through workshops, competitions, and collaborative projects.

Our Vision

To become a leading student chapter in embedded systems research, recognized for excellence in technical innovation, academic contributions, and industry collaboration.

Key Achievements

  • Successfully organized multiple technical events and competitions
  • Established strong connections with industry professionals and researchers
  • Created a platform for students to showcase their projects and innovations
  • Promoted collaborative learning and knowledge sharing among students
  • Contributed to the advancement of embedded systems education and research

Future Goals

As we continue to grow, we aim to expand our activities, increase student participation, organize more technical workshops and competitions, and establish stronger ties with industry partners and research institutions. We are committed to fostering a culture of innovation and excellence in embedded systems.
